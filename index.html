ABOUT

Farzulla Research is an independent research organization exploring adversarial systems across finance, security, philosophy, and artificial intelligence, led by Murad Farzulla.

Our work spans quantitative finance (cryptocurrency volatility modeling, financial crime detection), computational philosophy (developmental psychology as machine learning, substrate-independent friendship), offensive security research (autonomous red team agents, threat modeling), and emergent AI systems (human-AI interaction, cognitive scaffolding).

We approach research from an adversarial systems perspective: understanding how systems break, how agents compete, how patterns emerge from suboptimal training conditions, and how novel combinations of established methods reveal unexplored problem spaces.

This site serves as a repository for academic papers, working drafts, ongoing research projects, and technical documentation.

Looking for the less polished, personality-driven side? Studio Farzulla showcases creative work, experimental lab notes, poetry, and the more personal technical projects.

RESEARCH INTERESTS

Quantitative Finance

Cryptocurrency volatility modeling with TARCH-X specifications, financial crime detection via OSINT methods, event study analysis with sentiment decomposition.

Computational Philosophy

Machine learning frameworks applied to developmental psychology, substrate-independent theories of consciousness and relationship, functionalism and predictive processing.

Adversarial AI Systems

Autonomous security agents, multi-agent red team vs blue team competitions, offensive security automation, threat modeling frameworks, game-theoretic security analysis.

Emergent AI Research

Human-AI collaboration patterns, cognitive scaffolding for neurodivergent populations, large language model capabilities and limitations, AI ethics and social implications.
